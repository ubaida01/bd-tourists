Cox's Bazar

The world’s longest natural sandy sea beach

About Cox's Bazar

Cox’s Bazar is a beautiful coastal town in southeastern Bangladesh, famous for the longest uninterrupted sea beach in the world. Tourists flock here for the stunning views, golden sands, seafood, and cultural vibrancy. Don’t miss Inani Beach’s coral stones or a sunset drive along the scenic Marine Drive.

Key Attractions in Cox's Bazar

Inani Beach

Famous for coral stones, calm waters, and fewer crowds — perfect for a peaceful retreat.

Himchari Waterfall

A scenic natural waterfall with hilltop viewpoints and picnic spots just south of the city.

Marine Drive

A stunning coastal highway stretching from Cox’s Bazar to Teknaf — ideal for scenic drives.

Laboni Beach

The most popular and accessible beach area with local markets, food stalls, and vibrant activity.

Saint Martin’s Island

A small tropical island known for crystal-clear waters and coral life — reachable by boat.
